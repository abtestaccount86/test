Our Purpose:

Strategic Sourcing plays a crucial role in managing procurement activities, specifically focusing on Not For Resale (NFR) spend. This encompasses all expenditures related to goods and services that are not intended for resale but are essential for operations. Key focus areas include managing NFR spend, executing strategic projects, and driving cost savings.

NFR Spend Management:

Sourcing manages $2 Billion in annual NFR spend, with a focus in categories like office supplies, site services, IT services, parts & consumables, utilities, and more. We ensure C&S obtains high-quality goods and services at competitive prices through robust RFX processes and forward/reverse auctions. We work cross-functionally to identify opportunities in cost/savings, contract management, and process improvements.

Projects:

Strategic sourcing is deeply involved in project-based procurement. This involves collaborating with internal stakeholders to understand their specific needs and identifying the best suppliers to fulfill these requirements. The team manages the end-to-end procurement process for various projects, ensuring timely delivery, cost efficiency, and adherence to quality standards. This includes negotiating contracts, managing supplier relationships, and overseeing project timelines to ensure successful execution.

Savings:

Achieving cost savings is a fundamental objective of the strategic sourcing department. The team employs various strategies to drive savings, such as consolidating supplier bases, leveraging bulk purchasing, and negotiating favorable terms and conditions. By continuously analyzing spend data and market trends, the department identifies opportunities to reduce costs and enhance value.
